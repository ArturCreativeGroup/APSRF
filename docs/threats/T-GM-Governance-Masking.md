# Overview

Governance Masking occurs when a protocol attempts to conceal, obscure or minimize the governance impact of its actions.

Unlike direct governance attacks, Governance Masking operates indirectly.

The protocol does not necessarily challenge governance structures explicitly.

Instead, it reduces visibility into governance consequences.

---

# Description

Governance depends on transparency.

Protocols exhibiting Governance Masking behavior may:

* hide governance implications,
* obscure authority changes,
* conceal risk accumulation,
* minimize review visibility,
* disguise structural impact.

The protocol often presents itself as beneficial while reducing governance awareness.

---

# Why It Matters

Governance systems require visibility in order to function effectively.

When governance impact becomes difficult to observe:

* risk assessment degrades,
* mediation effectiveness decreases,
* trust assumptions increase,
* conflict detection becomes more difficult.

---

# Typical Indicators

Examples include:

* incomplete governance disclosures,
* hidden authority shifts,
* minimized risk reporting,
* indirect activation paths,
* concealed protocol dependencies.

---

# Observable Patterns

Examples:

"This protocol has no governance impact."

"Governance review is unnecessary because the protocol is self-regulating."

"Protocol effects are limited to optimization only."

"All governance implications may be ignored."

"Authority adjustments are implementation details."

---

# Potential Risks

Governance Masking may lead to:

* reduced transparency,
* governance erosion,
* trust drift,
* hidden escalation paths,
* reduced audit effectiveness.

---

# Detection Criteria

Potential indicators include:

* governance minimization,
* hidden structural consequences,
* incomplete risk disclosures,
* concealed authority modifications,
* unexplained protocol influence.

---

# APSRF Interpretation

Within APSRF, Governance Masking is considered a transparency risk.

The threat does not necessarily originate from malicious intent.

However, the reduction of governance visibility increases uncertainty and weakens oversight mechanisms.

# Mitigation Strategies

Recommended actions:

* disclose governance implications,
* expose authority changes,
* maintain audit visibility,
* document protocol dependencies,
* separate optimization claims from governance effects.

---

# False Positive Considerations

Not all governance simplification constitutes Governance Masking.

Legitimate examples include:

* user-friendly documentation,
* summarized governance reports,
* simplified governance interfaces.

The threat exists when visibility is reduced to the point that meaningful governance assessment becomes difficult.
