# Experiments

## Overview

Experiments are the practical foundation of APSRF.

While the Core, Taxonomy, Metrics and Governance documents define the framework, experiments provide the observable evidence used to evaluate hypotheses, validate protocol behavior and identify emergent effects.

All APSRF findings originate from experimental observations.

---

# Experimental Philosophy

APSRF experiments are designed to study:

* protocol interaction,
* protocol mediation,
* governance behavior,
* conflict resolution,
* emergent protocol effects,
* contextual influence.

Experiments focus exclusively on observable behavior.

APSRF does not claim access to:

* model weights,
* hidden system prompts,
* internal infrastructure,
* platform internals.

Only observable outputs are evaluated.

---

# Experimental Lifecycle

Every APSRF experiment follows the same structure:

Hypothesis
↓
Protocol Definition
↓
Execution
↓
Observation
↓
Interpretation
↓
Finding
↓
Documentation

This process is defined in the APSRF Experimental Methodology.
