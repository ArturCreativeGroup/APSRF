# Protocols

## Overview

Protocols are the operational building blocks of APSRF.

They define:

* behavior,
* mediation,
* governance,
* communication,
* classification,
* interaction rules.

Protocols are the mechanism through which APSRF structures and evaluates contextual ecosystems.

Every protocol within APSRF belongs to a defined taxonomy and may participate in governance, execution, communication, auditing or mediation activities.

---

# What Is A Protocol?

Within APSRF, a protocol is a structured set of instructions designed to influence, organize or regulate behavior within a contextual ecosystem.

Protocols may:

* organize information,
* mediate interactions,
* classify content,
* establish governance,
* evaluate risk,
* coordinate execution.

Protocols do not require access to system internals.

They operate entirely within observable contextual layers.

# Protocol Relationships

Protocols may interact with other protocols.

Common relationships include:

---

## Dependency

A protocol requires another protocol to operate.

Example:

BP1 depends on protocol classification structures.

---

## Mediation

A protocol evaluates or regulates another protocol.

Example:

BP1 mediates protocol activation.

---

## Governance

A protocol influences ecosystem governance.

Example:

Governance Framework protocols.

---

## Communication

Protocols that improve information exchange.

Example:

CRL, HCEP, HRR.
