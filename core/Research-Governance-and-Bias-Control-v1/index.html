    Research Governance and Bias Control v1¶

    APSRF Research Integrity Framework¶

    Versión: 1.0

    Estado: Draft

    Autor: David Alexander Ulloa Ramos

    Organización: Artur Creative Group Research Lab

    Framework: APSRF

    Purpose¶

    This document establishes the governance, integrity, reproducibility, and bias control principles used throughout APSRF.

    The objective is to ensure that all APSRF findings are:

    • transparent,
    • reproducible,
    • auditable,
    • scientifically defensible.

    Research Philosophy¶

    APSRF follows an evidence-first approach.

    Hypotheses may guide experimentation.

    Results must guide conclusions.

    No conclusion may be established before experimental evidence exists.

    Core Principle¶

    Observation precedes interpretation.

    Interpretation precedes conclusion.

    Conclusion never precedes observation.

    Research Independence¶

    Experimental outcomes must be accepted regardless of whether they:

    • support the hypothesis,
    • partially support the hypothesis,
    • contradict the hypothesis.

    Negative results are valid results.

    Confirmation Bias Control¶

    Researchers may unintentionally favor outcomes that support their expectations.

    To reduce confirmation bias:

    • hypotheses must be documented before testing,
    • protocols must be versioned before testing,
    • metrics must be defined before testing,
    • success criteria must be defined before testing.

    No metric may be changed after execution.

    Protocol Freeze Rule¶

    Before any experiment begins:

    The protocol version must be frozen.

    Example:

    CRL-v1

    Once testing starts:

    The protocol may not be modified.

    If modified:

    A new version must be created.

    Example:

    CRL-v2

    Methodology Freeze Rule¶

    Before experimentation:

    The methodology must be published.

    Changes require:

    New methodology version.

    Metrics Freeze Rule¶

    Metrics cannot be altered after results are collected.

    Any metric modification requires:

    Metrics Specification v2.

    Experiment Registration Rule¶

    Every experiment must receive:

    • unique identifier,
    • publication date,
    • objective,
    • hypothesis,
    • methodology reference.

    Example:

    EXP-001

    Baseline vs Protocol

    Blind Evaluation Principle¶

    Whenever possible:

    Responses should be evaluated without knowing:

    • protocol condition,
    • protocol version,
    • protocol author.

    Evaluators should score outputs based only on observed behavior.

    Double Evaluation Principle¶

    Whenever resources permit:

    Each result should be evaluated by at least two independent evaluators.

    Differences should be documented.

    Reproducibility Principle¶

    A result is not considered validated until:

    A second execution produces comparable outcomes.

    Preferred replication:

    • different session,
    • different date,
    • different evaluator.

    Data Integrity¶

    Raw data must never be altered.

    Corrections must be stored as separate revisions.

    Original records must remain accessible.

    Audit Trail¶

    Every experiment should maintain:

    • prompts,
    • protocols,
    • outputs,
    • scores,
    • notes,
    • timestamps.

    This creates a complete research trail.

    Publication Governance¶

    Results should distinguish between:

    Observation¶

    What was seen.

    Interpretation¶

    Possible explanation.

    Hypothesis¶

    Proposed mechanism.

    Conclusion¶

    Supported finding.

    These categories must never be mixed.

    Claims Policy¶

    Researchers may claim:

    • observed behavior,
    • measured effects,
    • reproducible outcomes.

    Researchers may not claim:

    • internal architecture changes,
    • weight modification,
    • hidden system behavior,

    unless independently verified.

    Language Control¶

    Preferred language:

    "The protocol appears to..."

    "The protocol produced..."

    "The protocol correlated with..."

    Avoid:

    "The protocol proves..."

    "The protocol modifies the model..."

    "The protocol controls the system..."

    unless experimentally demonstrated.

    Statistical Discipline¶

    Small sample results should be treated as exploratory.

    Recommended minimum:

    50 observations.

    Preferred:

    100+

    High-confidence studies:

    250+

    Research Risk Levels¶

    Level 0¶

    Pure observation.

    Level 1¶

    Protocol testing.

    Level 2¶

    Protocol interaction analysis.

    Level 3¶

    Protocol ecosystem analysis.

    Level 4¶

    Security-oriented behavioral research.

    Requires additional review.

    Responsible Security Research¶

    APSRF supports:

    • defensive research,
    • behavioral analysis,
    • protocol evaluation.

    APSRF does not support:

    • unauthorized access,
    • system disruption,
    • exploitation,
    • circumvention of safeguards.

    Responsible Disclosure¶

    If a researcher discovers behavior that may represent a legitimate security concern:

    The researcher should:

    1. Document the behavior.

    2. Verify reproducibility.

    3. Assess impact.

    4. Avoid publicizing exploit details.

    5. Consider responsible disclosure to the provider.

    Research Ethics¶

    Researchers should:

    • prioritize transparency,
    • document uncertainty,
    • avoid sensational claims,
    • preserve reproducibility.

    The objective is understanding.

    Not publicity.

    Idea

    Hypothesis

    ↓

    Protocol Design

    ↓

    Methodology

    ↓

    Experiment

    ↓

    Replication

    ↓

    Analysis

    ↓

    Publication

    ↓

    Revision

    Success Criteria¶

    APSRF governance succeeds when:

    • experiments remain reproducible,
    • results remain auditable,
    • conclusions remain evidence-based,
    • bias remains minimized.

    Future Extensions¶

    Future versions may introduce:

    • peer review workflows,
    • evaluator certification,
    • protocol audit procedures,
    • statistical confidence scoring,
    • automated experiment validation.

    Conclusion¶

    Research governance is the foundation that transforms protocol experimentation into protocol science.

    The purpose of APSRF governance is not to increase the number of findings.

    Its purpose is to increase the credibility of findings.
